What Is BMI?

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a simple measurement using your height and weight to estimate body fat. It's widely used by healthcare professionals as a quick screening tool to identify potential weight-related health risks in adults.

BMI Categories

BMI RangeCategoryHealth Risk
Below 18.5UnderweightNutritional deficiency risk
18.5 – 24.9Normal weightLow risk (healthy range)
25.0 – 29.9OverweightModerate health risk
30.0 and aboveObeseHigh health risk

Limitations of BMI

BMI is a useful screening tool but has limitations. It doesn't directly measure body fat and may misclassify athletes (high muscle mass) as overweight, or elderly people (low muscle mass) as normal weight. Always consult a healthcare professional for a comprehensive health assessment.
