Why Does Domain Age Matter?

Domain age is a factor in SEO because older domains have often built up more backlinks, authority, and trust with search engines over time. Google considers domain history as a signal — a domain that has been consistently active for many years is generally more trusted than a brand-new one.

What Is WHOIS?

WHOIS is a protocol used to query databases that store registered domain names, IP address blocks, and autonomous systems. It provides registration information including creation date, expiry date, registrar name, and sometimes registrant contact details. Many registrars offer "WHOIS privacy" services that hide personal information.

How Domain Age Affects SEO

While domain age alone is not a major ranking factor, the history of a domain matters. An aged domain with a clean history (no penalties, consistent content) typically ranks faster than a brand-new domain in competitive niches. When buying a domain, always check its history and ensure it hasn't been penalized by Google.
